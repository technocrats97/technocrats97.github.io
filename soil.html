Types of Soils Included In Map

Laterite soil- Lack fertility due to intensive leaching When manured and irrigated, some laterites are suitable for growing plantation crops like tea, coffee, rubber, cinchona, coconut.

Mountain soil - They are deficient in potash, phosphorus and lime.

Desert soil - Phosphates and nitrates make these soil fertile wherever moisture is available.

Alkaline soil - The accumulation of these salts makes the soil infertile and renders it unfit for agriculture.

Marshy soil - Most of these soils are under water during the rainy season but as soon the rains cease, they should be put under paddy cultivation.

Aluvial soil- The nutrient content of new alluvial soil is fair to medium depending on how long it has been cultivated. Conversely, the nutrient content of old alluvial soils is very low.

Red soil- They are acidic mainly due to the nature of the parent rocks and are poor in lime, magnesia, phosphates, nitrogen and humus.

Black soil- This soil contains moderate amounts of phosphorous but is poor in nitrogen.
